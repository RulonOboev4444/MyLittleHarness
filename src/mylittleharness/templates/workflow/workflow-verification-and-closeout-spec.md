# Workflow Verification And Closeout Spec

This stable workflow spec fixture anchors the expected verification and closeout surface for a MyLittleHarness operating root.

Repo-visible project files remain authoritative. This fixture is repair-created only when absent, and it cannot approve repair, closeout, archive, commit, rollback, lifecycle decisions, or future mutations.

Verified phase work stops at repo-visible state/evidence unless the active plan explicitly opts into `auto_continue` and records stop-condition coverage. Closeout preparation remains an explicit boundary, not an automatic side effect of passing checks. `writeback --active-phase <next-phase> --phase-status pending` is the explicit same-plan phase-advance rail; `writeback --phase-status complete` is a ready-for-closeout boundary only. A same-request `docs_decision = uncertain` plus `phase_status = complete` may replace mismatched prior closeout facts with current plan identity and provisional docs posture, but it still cannot carry stale closeout facts or approve archive, roadmap done-status, source-incubation archive, or next-slice opening. After a plan is already archived, `writeback --roadmap-item <id> --archived-plan <project/archive/plans/...>` may refresh same-request closeout, roadmap, and source-incubation facts for that archived identity without reopening lifecycle state. Lifecycle-field writeback that would also trigger project-state auto-compaction refuses before writing unless the operator explicitly supplies `--allow-auto-compaction` after review or runs `writeback --compact-only` as a separate maintenance step.

Read-only check and grain diagnostics may flag missing, generic, unresolved, or toolchain-mismatched verification gates, adjacent regression-test ownership gaps, raw-log-heavy plans, done roadmap items without archived evidence, and weak archived-plan closeout samples. These findings are closeout prompts and calibration evidence only; they cannot mark work verified, write closeout state, archive a plan, or change roadmap status.
